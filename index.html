Price vs Satisfaction Analysis

This visualization compares game prices with player satisfaction scores. Each bubble represents a game, where larger bubbles indicate larger player bases. It helps answer whether expensive games truly provide better player experiences.

What Does The Data Actually Tell Us?

The visualizations consistently reveal a surprising trend: larger and more expensive multiplayer games attract massive audiences, but they often struggle to maintain high player satisfaction. Meanwhile, lower-cost singleplayer and indie experiences frequently achieve much stronger player approval ratings.

🎯 Multiplayer Burnout

Games like Battlefield and large online shooters attract millions of players, but server issues, balancing problems, and toxic competition reduce satisfaction.

⭐ Indie Excellence

Smaller games focus heavily on gameplay quality and creativity, which results in much higher player satisfaction scores.

💸 Price vs Expectations

As game prices increase, player expectations become harder to satisfy. Premium pricing often leads to harsher criticism from players.
